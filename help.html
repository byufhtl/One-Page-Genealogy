Getting Started

One Page Genealogy is the perfect site to create a compact tree chart of your family with a few quick clicks.

Create Your Chart

You can create a chart from two basic sources: a Gedcom file or FamilySearch.

From a Gedcom

  • Once you have logged in, click "Read Gedcom File" if you want to create a chart from a Gedcom file you have on your computer.

  • Select your Gedcom file using the window that pops up and click open.

  • Choose id of the person who will be at the beginning of the chart.

  • Choose the type of chart you want and how many generations back you want the chart to go.

    • Ascendancy: creates a tree with each generation being the parents of the last generation.
    • Descendancy: creates a tree with each generation being the children of the last generation by spouse.
  • Click "Save Chart" to create the chart. It may take a few minutes for the chart to appear.
  • If you click "Cancel" you can click the "Gedcom" or "FamilySearch" buttons at the top of the page to restart the chart creation process.

From FamilySearch

  • Once you log in, click "FamilySearch" if you want to create a chart from data found on FamilySearch.

  • Enter the FamilySearch id (XXXX-XXX) of the person who will be at the beginning of the chart or leave it empty to start with yourself.

  • Choose the type of chart you want and how many generations back you want the chart to go.

    • Ascendancy: creates a tree with each generation being the parents of the last generation.
    • Descendancy: creates a tree with each generation being the children of the last generation by spouse.
  • Click "Submit" to create the chart. You will be prompted to log into FamilySearch. Once you've logged in, it may take a few minutes for the chart to appear.
  • If you click "Cancel" you can click the "Gedcom" or "FamilySearch" buttons at the top of the page to restart the chart creation process.

Explore Your Chart

Now you have your chart on the page, but maybe it's a bit too big to see all of it.

  • Zoom in and out: scroll middle mouse button.

  • Pan around: left click and drag the chart.

  • Click the arrows at the top of the screen to rotate your chart clockwise or counter-clockwise.

Edit Your Chart

There are a few things you can do to edit how your chart looks.

Changing Main Style

  • Click "Styles" at the top right of the page and then click the style you want.

    • The upper options are presets that will change the style of each box on the chart to fit the chosen style. Descendancy styles will correctly display descendancy charts.

    • The lower options are color presets that can be selected separately from the styles and will only change the colors of the boxes.

Changing Individual Box or Generation Styles

  • Click the box you wish to edit.

  • Use the drop down menu to preview any of the box styles.

  • Select if you want to just change the box you clicked ("Individual") or everyone in the generation selected ("Generation").

  • Click "Collapse" if you don't want ancestors/descendants of that individual to be shown. Clicking this can reduce the size of your chart.

  • Click "Save Changes" to have the changes made to your chart or "Cancel" to leave the menu without changing anything.

How to Print or save as a PDF

Here is the current way of creating a PDF that you can edit and make the size you desire:

  • Zoom out on the chart until your whole chart can be seen on your browser page.

  • Drag the chart over to be as close to the left side of the screen as possible.

 

If you are using Google Chrome:

  • Right click the chart and click "Print"

  • If you want a PDF, make sure the Destination is "Save as PDF." If it isn't, just click "Change..." and select the "Save as PDF" option. This way, you can edit the chart in any PDF editor and make the chart more readable if needed.

 

If you are using Firefox:

  • At the top on the right of the browser click the icon with three horizontal bars.

  • Click print, then print again at the top of the new page that new pops up.

  • If you want a PDF, click "Print to File" at the top of the printer options. This way, you can edit the chart in any PDF editor and make the chart more readable if needed.

Getting Started

One Page Genealogy is the perfect site to create a compact tree chart of your family with a few quick clicks.

Create Your Chart

You can create a chart from two basic sources: a Gedcom file or FamilySearch.

Note: You can always click the "Gedcom" or "FamilySearch" buttons at the top of the page to restart the chart creation process.

From a Gedcom

  • Once you have logged in, click "Read Gedcom File" if you want to create a chart from a Gedcom file you have on your computer.

  • Select your Gedcom file using the window that pops up and click open.

  • Choose id of the person who will be at the beginning of the chart.

  • Choose the type of chart you want and how many generations back you want the chart to go.

    • Ascendancy: creates a tree with each generation being the parents of the last generation.
    • Descendancy: creates a tree with each generation being the children of the last generation by spouse.
  • Click "Save Chart" to create the chart. It may take a few minutes for the chart to appear.

From FamilySearch

  • Click "FamilySearch" if you want to create a chart from data found on FamilySearch.

  • Enter the FamilySearch id (XXXX-XXX) of the person who will be at the beginning of the chart or leave it empty to start with yourself.

  • Choose the type of chart you want (Descendancy of Ascendancy) and how many generations you want displayed in the chart.

    • Ascendancy: creates a tree with each generation being the parents of the previous generation.
    • Descendancy: creates a tree with each generation being the children of the previous generation by spouse.
  • If you have not already authenticated with FamilySearch:
    • You will be prompted to do so through FamilySearch login.
    • This is necessary because all the data for your tree is pulled straight from FamilySearch.
  • If you've previously authenticated with FamilySearch through One Page Genealogy, simply press "Submit" to generate your chart.
  • Because of the large amount of data, may take a few minutes for the chart to appear.
  • Expect longer loading times for larger numbers of generations.

Explore Your Chart

Now you have your chart on the page, but maybe it's a bit too big to see all of it.

  • Zoom in and out: scroll middle mouse button.

  • Pan around: left click and drag the chart.

  • Click the arrows at the top of the screen to rotate your chart clockwise or counter-clockwise.

Edit Your Chart

There are a few things you can do to edit how your chart looks.

Changing Main Style

  • Click "Styles" at the top right of the page and then choose the style you want.

    • The upper options will change the style of every box on the chart to fit the chosen style. Descendancy styles will correctly display descendancy charts.

    • The lower options can be selected separately from the styles and will only change the colors of the boxes.

Customize Box Styles

  • Click the box you wish to edit.

  • Use the drop down menu to preview any of the box styles.

  • Select if you want to just change the box you clicked ("Individual"), everyone in that generation ("Generation"), or everyone in the entire chart ("Entire Chart").

  • Click "Save Changes" to have the changes made to your chart or "Cancel" to leave the menu without changing anything.

How to Print or save as a PDF

Simply press the download button (downward point arrow icon) at the top right to save your chart as a PDF.

You can then take the downloaded PDF to a nearby print service center.
